Just for Luke & Dana

Leo’s little guide

Hi Mum & Dad. This is Leo’s family page—a private spot for everyone who loves him to follow along and send their love. Here’s everything you can do, kept dead simple for the days when you’re running on no sleep. 🤍

🔒 First things first — this page is private

Leo’s page is not public. It’s locked behind a family password, it won’t show up on Google, and the only people who can ever see it are the ones you and Amy share the password with. Share it as widely or as carefully as you like—it’s completely yours.

This little guide is locked even tighter—only you two can open it, with your parent code.

🔑 Right now, it’s just you two

At this moment, you are the only people who can see this site—not even the family who’ve sent their video messages have been let in yet. It’s sitting here quietly, waiting for your go-ahead.

Whenever you’re ready for the family to come and see it:

  1. Just say the word to Amy.
  2. She’ll send everyone who’s shared a video the family access password.
  3. They use it to come in—and they land straight on the home page with all the photos, videos and milestones. (This guide stays private to you two—they never see it.)

No rush at all. The family only gets in when you’re happy for them to—it’s entirely your call.

1 The quickest way to update everyone

Open the Milestones tab and tap “Leo’s daily check-in”. Tap how he’s doing, tap today’s little wins, add his weight if you like, and post. That’s it—the whole family sees it instantly. No need to think of the right words.

Best habit: one tiny check-in a day, even just a mood and one win. The family treasures the small stuff—a good feed, an open eye, a quiet cuddle. It doesn’t have to be momentous.

2 Adding a proper milestone

For the big moments (off the CPAP, first bath, coming home), use “Add a milestone” on the Milestones tab. Pop in the date, a short title, the story, and a photo or video if you have one—those lovely details are what everyone treasures. Add an emoji to give it some character.

  • Milestones sort themselves into date order automatically.
  • You can add them any time, even days later—just set the right date.
  • Write naturally; little jokes are welcome (“1ml of milk—basically a Big Mac for him!”).

3 Photos & videos from the family

On the Feed tab, the family posts photos and short video messages for Leo—hellos from grandparents, aunties, uncles and cousins all over the world. There are gentle writing prompts if anyone’s stuck on what to say, and you can comment under any post or milestone to keep the conversation going.

4 The family tree & your names

The Family tab shows everyone who loves Leo, from Cayman to New Zealand and beyond. Tap anyone to see what they’ve shared. Grandparents can tap their own card to choose what Leo will call them (Nana, Poppa, and so on).

5 Anything else?

If you ever want something changed—a new milestone written up, a different photo featured, the password swapped, a feature added—just tell Amy and she’ll sort it. This page is yours; it can grow with Leo.
